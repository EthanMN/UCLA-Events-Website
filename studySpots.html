Adjusting to classes as a first-year can be overwhelming! Here are some of the best spots many Bruins use to study!

Northern Lights Cafe is a cozy hub in UCLA's North Campus student center offering coffee, pastries, sushi, and frozen yogurt. With a beautiful fireplace and comfortable seating, it's a perfect spot to study and relax.

The Study in Hedrick Hall is a quiet and comfortable space with large windows and a fireplace. It offers a wide variety of food options from pizza to sandwiches, making it a great place to grab a snack and focus on assignments.

Kerckhoff Hall has the essence of a castle and sits beautifully in the middle of campus. With a coffee shop and music nearby, it’s a great area to study after class.

Powell Library is the main library on campus and offers a variety of study spaces. It has a large collection of books and resources, quiet study areas, and group study rooms for collaborative work.

The Bombshelter is a comfortable space in the basement of the College of Letters and Science. Despite its name, it offers many dining options and is a great place to study and relax away from the hustle and bustle of campus life.
