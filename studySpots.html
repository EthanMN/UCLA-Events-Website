Adjusting to classes as a first-year can be overwhelming! Here are some of the best spots many Bruins use to study!

Northern Lights Cafe is a counter-serve hub in UCLA's North Campus student center for coffee, pastries, sushi, and frozen yogurt. With a beautiful fireplace and comfortable seating, it's a perfect spot to study and relax.

The Study in Hedrick Hall is a quiet, comfortable space with a large window and a fireplace. Additionally it has a wide variety of food options ranging from pizza to sandwiches. It's a great place to grab a snack, study, and focus on assignments.

Kerckhoff has the essence of a castle and is a beautiful spot in the middle of campus. With a coffee shop and music, this is a great area to study after class.

Powell Library is the main library on campus and has a variety of study spaces. With a large collection of books and resources, it's a great place to find information for research papers and projects. Additionally, it has quiet study areas and group study rooms for collaborative work.

The Bombshelter is a quiet, comfortable space in the basement of the College of Letters and Science. Despite it's name, it has many restaurants and is a great place to study and relax away from the hustle and bustle of campus life.
